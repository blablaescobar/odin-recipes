Wasabi Ice Cream

Description

Experience a bold fusion of heat and chill with our Wasabi Ice Cream. Crafted with creamy dairy goodness and infused with authentic wasabi paste, each scoop delivers a tantalizing balance of fiery spice and cool refreshment. Prepare your taste buds for an unforgettable adventure in every bite.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups heavy cream
  • 1 cup whole milk
  • 3/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 4 large egg yolks
  • 2 tablespoons wasabi paste
  • Optional: Wasabi peas for garnish

Steps

  1. In a saucepan, combine the heavy cream, whole milk, and granulated sugar. Heat the mixture over medium heat, stirring occasionally, until it reaches a simmer. Remove from heat.
  2. In a separate bowl, whisk the egg yolks until smooth and pale in color.
  3. Slowly pour the hot cream mixture into the bowl with the egg yolks, whisking continuously to temper the eggs.
  4. Once combined, return the mixture to the saucepan and place it back on the stove over low heat. Cook the mixture, stirring constantly, until it thickens slightly and coats the back of a spoon, about 5-7 minutes. Do not let it boil.
  5. Remove the saucepan from the heat and stir in the wasabi paste until thoroughly incorporated. Adjust the amount of wasabi paste to your desired level of spiciness.
  6. Strain the mixture through a fine-mesh sieve into a clean bowl to remove any lumps or solids.
  7. Cover the bowl with plastic wrap, ensuring the wrap touches the surface of the custard to prevent a skin from forming. Refrigerate the custard for at least 4 hours or overnight until thoroughly chilled.
  8. Once chilled, transfer the custard to an ice cream maker and churn according to the manufacturer's instructions until it reaches a soft-serve consistency.
  9. If desired, fold in some crushed wasabi peas for an extra burst of flavor and texture.
  10. Transfer the churned ice cream to a freezer-safe container and freeze for an additional 2-4 hours, or until firm.
  11. Serve the wasabi ice cream scoops in bowls or cones, garnishing with additional wasabi peas if desired.
  12. Enjoy the unique and refreshing flavor of homemade wasabi ice cream!

Note: Wasabi ice cream is best enjoyed fresh but can be stored in the freezer for up to 1 week. Allow it to soften slightly at room temperature for a few minutes before serving.
